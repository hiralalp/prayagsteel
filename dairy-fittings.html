Angular Valve

These valves are Manual Operated valves to regulate the flow anywhere from full open to fully close. These are designed specifically to accurately control the process. Improved sealing devices and highly accurate machining of the screw rod (stem) have provided tight shutoff as well as characterizable control. The design of the regulating element allows the stem holder to turn into the flow without any significant side loads. The linear action of the screw rod provides tight fit against the seal, which allows for clean separation of the process during closure. Tight shutoff is a characteristic of angular valves.

Floating Ball Valve

The floating ball valve is a widely used type of quarter-turn valve designed for precise flow control in various industrial applications. Its unique design features a ball with a through-hole that rotates within the valve body to control the flow of liquids or gases. The ball is not fixed to the stem, allowing it to "float" between the upstream and downstream seats. This floating action enables the ball to move slightly under pressure, ensuring a tight seal against the downstream seat. Floating ball valves are known for their excellent sealing performance, durability, and ease of operation.

Trunnion Mounted Ball Valve

A trunnion mounted ball valve is a robust and advanced type of quarter-turn valve designed for high-pressure and large-diameter applications. Unlike floating ball valves, the ball in a trunnion mounted valve is anchored at the top and bottom by trunnions (shafts), which prevents the ball from moving under pressure. This fixed position allows the valve seats to move and press against the ball, creating a reliable seal even at high pressures. These valves are ideal for demanding applications in industries such as oil and gas, petrochemical plants, power generation, and pipeline systems.
